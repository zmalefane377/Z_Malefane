Week 6: Laser Cutting

Date: 4th- 18th November 2025

Quick Summary

  • Skills Learned: Proficient in laser cutting, including digital design, machine operation, material handling, and safe production of precision components.
  • Digital Tools and Software Used: Digital tools and software used include CorelDRAW and AutoCAD for vector design, technical drawings, and laser cutting preparation.
  • Topics: Key topics covered in laser cutting include laser technology fundamentals, digital design and file preparation, machine operation and calibration, safety procedures, and quality control.
  • Key Concepts: laser principles, vector design, precision, calibration, safety, and quality control.
  • Challenges Faced: Challenges faced included machine calibration issues, material inconsistencies, design-to-output alignment errors, and maintaining consistent cut quality while adhering to safety requirements.
 

Introduction

Laser cutting is a modern digital fabrication process used to produce precise and high-quality components across various industries. This introduction outlines the foundational concepts of laser cutting, focusing on digital design, machine operation, and safe production practices. The experience emphasizes the development of practical technical skills, an understanding of laser technology principles, and the ability to translate digital designs into accurate physical outputs while maintaining quality and safety standards.

Kerf Testing

  1. Design a kerf set up according to the size of the wood you will use...in this case we used Coreldraw for the Design
  2. If you are unsure of the size you can measure it
  3. Make sure you have different hairlines or line measurements so that you can see which one is better to use on the material you will use
    1. The hairline
    2. The 0.5pt
  4. Cut out the pieces and note down the analysis for later reference
  5. Fit in the Wood piece to see where it best fits
