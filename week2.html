Week 2: Design Phase

Date: 7th-14th October 2025

Quick Summary

  • Skills Learned: Ideating, sketching, and creating digital designs using CAD while manipulating raster images for precise technical development.
  • Digital Tools and Software Used: Corel Draw and Gimp
  • Topics: Design ideation, sketching, CAD basics, and raster image manipulation.
  • Key Concepts: Translating ideas into sketches, creating simple CAD models, and using raster images to support accurate design development.
  • Challenges Faced: Converting ideas into sketches, mastering CAD tools, and effectively manipulating raster images for accurate designs.
 

Introduction

In week two, we focused on ideating and illustrating our design concepts while exploring the basics of raster manipulation. We learned how to express ideas through sketching and began using CAD tools to create simple digital representations of our designs. The session also introduced raster images, showing how they can be imported, adjusted, or traced within CAD software to support accurate design development. This combination of creativity, digital illustration, and image manipulation provided a strong foundation for moving from conceptual ideas to structured technical designs.

Picture manipulation using Gimp

  1. Installed Gimp
  2. https://www.gimp.org/downloads/
  3. Use the above link to download Gimp
  4. You will a picture to manipulate...should be any picture or design of your choice
  5. Make sure you have your desired image settings
  6. Insert the picture you want to manipulate
  7. Edit the picture settings in advanced, play around with the resolutions...Set the pixels to the units you comprehend best
  8. As you zoom in you will ee that a raster has been formed
  • Congratulations you can now manipulate a picture using Gimp

    • Picture manipulation using Corel DRAW

    1. Open corel DRAW and create a new document...edit the settings as you desire
    2. Import the picture you want to manipulate
    3. Zooming in you will see that your picture has no raster as shown below
    4. Go to Bitmaps and resample
    5. Play around with the resolution setting
    6. Zoom in your picture to see the raster
  • Congratulations you can now manipulate a picture using Corel DRAW
